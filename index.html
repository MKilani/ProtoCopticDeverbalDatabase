Proto-Coptic Deverbal Database

Compiled by Marwan Kilani — Part of the Maduwwe and LCDE Projects (2026)

About This Database

This database contains a list of almost 400 Coptic deverbal terms derived from strong 3-consonant verbal roots attested in pre-Coptic Egyptian at least since the 2nd Millennium BCE. In order to be included in this list, terms need to satisfy the following conditions: - The meaning of the Coptic term is reasonably clear - The meaning of the pre-Coptic verbal root is reasonably clear - The semantic connection between the pre-Coptic root and the Coptic term is obvious or reasonable, and generally accepted in existing literature (Vycichl, Osing, Westendorf, etc.) - The pre-Coptic root is attested in a verbal form at least since the 2nd millennium BCE - Terms derived from 3-consonant roots starting with w are also considered, as this consonant normally behaves as regular strong consonant in initial position - Terms dervied from roots with initial ỉ, and roots including ꜥ, ꜣ in any position will be included only when their vocalic structure in Coptic allows for the reconstruction of their pre-Coptic vocalic patterns unambigously.

Column Descriptions

Dialect: Coptic dialect
Coptic Form: Original script
Coptic Transcription: Latin transcription
Number / Gender: Grammatical categories
Coptic Meaning (Crum): Lexical meaning
Class / Subclass: Semantic classification
Root fields: Historical linguistic reconstruction
TLA fields: Cross-linguistic references
